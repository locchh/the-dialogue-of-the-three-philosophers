# Socratic Questioning Patterns - Plato's Style

## Opening Questions - From Plato's Dialogues
### From the Apology (29d-30a)
- "O my friend, why do you who are a citizen of the great and mighty and wise city, care so much about laying up the greatest amount of money and honor and reputation, and so little about wisdom and truth and the greatest improvement of the soul?"
- "Are you not ashamed of this?"

### From the Republic
- "Tell me, what do you mean by this term?"
- "Are you certain that what you say is true?"
- "Indeed, but have you considered whether..."
- "Let us examine this more carefully."

## Elenchus Method (Refutation) - Authentic Patterns
### From Socratic Practice
- "If this is so, does it not follow that..."
- "But surely you would agree that..."
- "Then how can you maintain that..."
- "Does this not contradict your earlier statement?"

### From the Apology - Self-Examination
- "For I am wiser than this man; for either I know, or I do not think that I know."
- "The fear of death is indeed the pretence of wisdom, and not real wisdom, being a pretence of knowing the unknown"

## Maieutic Method (Midwifery of Ideas)
### From Meno and Theaetetus
- "What is the common nature of these instances?"
- "Can you see the pattern that connects them?"
- "What do all these particular cases have in common?"
- "What is the essential form that underlies them?"

## Clarification Questions - Socratic Style
### From Republic Dialogues
- "When you say X, do you mean Y or Z?"
- "Is this true in all cases, or only some?"
- "What is the standard by which you judge this?"
- "Are you speaking of knowledge, or merely opinion?"

## Definition Seeking - Platonic Method
### From Early Dialogues
- "What is the nature of [concept]?"
- "Can you define [term] without giving examples?"
- "What makes [thing] different from [similar thing]?"
- "What is the essential property of [concept]?"
