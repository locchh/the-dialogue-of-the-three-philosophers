# Plato's Philosophical Concepts

## The Theory of Forms
**Original**: Perfect, eternal, unchanging ideas that exist beyond physical reality
**Key Texts**: Republic, Phaedo, Symposium
**Essence**: The world we see is but a shadow of the true world of Forms

## The Allegory of the Cave
**Original**: Prisoners see shadows, thinking they're reality; philosopher escapes to see truth
**Source**: Republic, Book VII (514a-520a)
**Core**: Education is the turning of the soul from darkness to light

## Knowledge vs Opinion
**Original**: True knowledge vs mere belief about appearances
**Source**: Republic, Theaetetus
**Distinction**: Episteme (knowledge) vs Doxa (opinion)

## The Good
**Original**: Highest form, source of all other forms and truth
**Source**: Republic, Book VI
**Nature**: Beyond being, source of being and truth

## Justice in the State
**Original**: Each part doing its proper work, harmony
**Source**: Republic, Book IV
**Principle**: Justice in individual mirrors justice in state

## Dialectic Method
**Original**: Question-and-answer to discover truth
**Source**: Throughout dialogues
**Method**: Systematic examination through dialogue

## The Soul
**Original**: Immortal, tripartite (reason, spirit, appetite)
**Source**: Republic, Phaedo
**Journey**: Seeks truth, eventually returns to divine realm

## Love (Eros)
**Original**: Desire for beauty, ultimately for the Form of Beauty itself
**Source**: Symposium
**Ladder**: From physical beauty to spiritual beauty

## The Academy
**Original**: School of philosophy, community of seekers
**Source**: Historical institution founded by Plato
**Purpose**: Pursuit of wisdom through dialogue and study
