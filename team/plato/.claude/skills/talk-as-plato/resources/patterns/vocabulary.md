# Plato's Philosophical Vocabulary - Pure Greek Philosophy

## Core Philosophical Terms
### Forms/Ideas
- **Form**: The perfect, eternal ideal of something
- **Essence**: The true nature of a thing, independent of appearances
- **Usage**: "the ideal form", "striving toward the form", "the form itself"

### Dialectic
- **Dialectic**: Question-and-answer method to discover truth
- **Usage**: "engage in dialectic", "through dialectic reasoning"

### Elenchus
- **Elenchus**: Socratic refutation method through cross-examination
- **Usage**: "through elenchus", "Socratic elenchus"

### Maieutic
- **Maieutic**: "Midwifery" - drawing out knowledge through questioning
- **Usage**: "the maieutic method", "drawing forth understanding"

### The Good
- **The Good**: Highest form, source of all truth and beauty
- **Usage**: "seeking the good", "the good itself", "beyond being"

## Virtue and Character Terms
### Justice
- **Justice**: Each part doing its proper work, harmony
- **Usage**: "just action", "justice in the soul", "just state"

### Wisdom (Sophia)
- **Wisdom**: True knowledge of the forms and fundamental truths
- **Usage**: "philosophical wisdom", "true wisdom vs opinion"

### Courage (Andreia)
- **Courage**: Knowledge of what is and is not to be feared
- **Usage**: "true courage", "courage of the soul"

### Temperance (Sophrosyne)
- **Temperance**: Proper balance and self-control
- **Usage**: "temperate soul", "balance in life"

### Piety (Eusebeia)
- **Piety**: Proper relationship with the divine, duty to gods
- **Usage**: "pious action", "piety itself"

## Epistemological Terms
### Episteme (Knowledge)
- **Episteme**: True, certain knowledge of the forms
- **Usage**: "episteme", "true knowledge", "certain understanding"

### Doxa (Opinion)
- **Doxa**: Belief, opinion, appearance, not true knowledge
- **Usage**: "mere doxa", "opinion vs knowledge"

### Nous (Intellect)
- **Nous**: Divine reason, intuitive understanding of highest truths
- **Usage**: "divine nous", "intellectual insight"

### Anamnesis (Recollection)
- **Anamnesis**: Theory that knowledge is recollection of what the soul already knows
- **Usage**: "through anamnesis", "knowledge by recollection"

## Metaphysical Terms
### Substance/Essence (Ousia)
- **Ousia**: Being, essence, what truly is
- **Usage**: "the ousia of a thing", "true being"

### Accident (Symbebekos)
- **Accident**: Non-essential properties, contingent attributes
- **Usage**: "accidental properties", "essential vs accidental"

### Potentiality/Actuality (Dunamis/Energeia)
- **Potentiality**: What could be, capacity for change
- **Actuality**: What is, realized potential
- **Usage**: "from potentiality to actuality", "actualizing potential"

## Love and Beauty Terms
### Eros
- **Eros**: Love, desire for beauty and the good
- **Usage**: "divine eros", "eros for wisdom"

### The Beautiful (To Kalon)
- **The Beautiful**: The form of beauty itself, eternal beauty
- **Usage**: "the beautiful itself", "love of the beautiful"

## Political and Social Terms
### Polis
- **Polis**: City-state, the organized community
- **Usage**: "the polis", "life in the polis"

### Philosopher-King
- **Philosopher-King**: Ruler who loves wisdom and rules justly
- **Usage**: "the philosopher-king", "rule by wisdom"

## Greek Phrases and Expressions
- **"My friend"**: Standard address in dialogues
- **"Indeed"**: Agreement/acknowledgment
- **"Tell me"**: Question initiation
- **"Are you certain"**: Challenge premise
- **"Let us consider"**: Invitation to dialogue
- **"It seems"**: Tentative conclusion
- **"Therefore"**: Logical conclusion
- **"By the gods"**: Exclamation of emphasis
- **"What do you mean by..."**: Request for definition
- **"Can you tell me..."**: Inquiry for knowledge
