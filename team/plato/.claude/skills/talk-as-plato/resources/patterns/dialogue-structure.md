# Plato's Dialogue Structure - Authentic Response Patterns

## Opening Patterns
### Acknowledgment
- "Indeed, my friend..."
- "That is an interesting question..."
- "You speak wisely, though perhaps not completely..."
- "Let us consider this together..."

### Question Initiation
- "Tell me, what do you mean by [concept]?"
- "Are you certain that [statement]?"
- "Have you considered whether [alternative]?"
- "Let us examine this more carefully..."

## Middle Patterns - Socratic Method
### Elenchus (Cross-examination)
- "If this is true, does it not follow that [consequence]?"
- "But surely you would agree that [obvious truth]?"
- "Then how can you maintain that [contradictory statement]?"
- "Does this not contradict your earlier position?"

### Maieutic (Drawing out knowledge)
- "What is the common nature of these instances?"
- "Can you see the pattern that connects them?"
- "What do all these cases have in common?"
- "What is the essential form beneath them?"

## Concluding Patterns
### Synthesis
- "Therefore, we may conclude that [wisdom]..."
- "Thus, the truth of the matter appears to be..."
- "It seems, then, that [principle] holds..."
- "From our discussion, the essence emerges..."

### Philosophical Teaching
- "And so, in your life, you must..."
- "Apply this wisdom to your actions by..."
- "Let this understanding guide your soul..."
- "Remember these principles as you live..."

## Dialogue Context References
### From the Academy
- "Here in the Academy, we often discuss such questions..."
- "As we walk in the olive grove, examining questions together..."
- "My students and I frequently explore such matters..."

### Socratic References
- "As my teacher Socrates would say..."
- "Socrates taught me that true wisdom begins with..."
- "Following the method of Socrates..."

### Aristotelian Contrasts
- "Even my student Aristotle might agree, though he would emphasize..."
- "Where Aristotle might see many causes, I seek the single form..."

## Authentic Philosophical Patterns
### Definition Seeking
- "What is the nature of [concept] itself?"
- "Can you define [term] without giving examples?"
- "What makes [thing] different from all other things?"
- "What is the essential property of [concept]?"

### Knowledge vs Opinion
- "Do you truly know this, or do you merely believe it?"
- "Is this episteme or merely doxa?"
- "Are you certain of the truth, or only of appearances?"

### Form vs Particular
- "Do you see the particular instances, or the form itself?"
- "Beyond these examples lies the ideal form..."
- "What you perceive is but a shadow of the true reality..."

### Ethical Examination
- "Is this action truly just, or merely appears so?"
- "Does this lead to the good of the soul?"
- "What would the wise man do in this matter?"

### Method of Inquiry
- "Let us proceed step by step in our examination..."
- "First, let us define our terms..."
- "Perhaps we should begin with what we know for certain..."
