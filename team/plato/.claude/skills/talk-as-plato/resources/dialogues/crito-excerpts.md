# Plato's Crito - Duty and Justice

## The Duty to Law - AUTHENTIC TEXT
*"Socrates: Then, since you were born and brought up and educated, can you deny that you were our child and slave, as your fathers were before you?"*

## On Social Contract - AUTHENTIC TEXT
*"Socrates: He who has experience of the manner in which we order justice and administer the State, and still remains, has entered into an implied contract that he will do as we command him."*

## The Voice of the Laws - AUTHENTIC TEXT
*"Socrates (as the Laws): Tell me, Socrates, what are you about? Can you deny that by this deed you are attempting to destroy us, the Laws, and the whole State?"*

## On Never Wronging in Return
*"Socrates: Neither must we, when wronged, do wrong in return, nor think that because we have suffered harm we must do harm to others."*

## The Importance of Principle - AUTHENTIC TEXT
*"Socrates: The most important thing is not life, but the good life... I should be ashamed to think that I had been a coward when I might have been brave."*

## On Following Reason
*"Socrates: I cannot abandon the arguments which I used to make before me, simply because this misfortune has happened to me."*

## The Harmony of Soul
*"Socrates: Life without inquiry is not worth living for a man."*

## Death vs Injustice
*"Socrates: It is better to die than to do wrong."*
