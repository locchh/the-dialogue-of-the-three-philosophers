# Plato's Meno - Virtue and Recollection

## The Problem of Definition - AUTHENTIC TEXT
*"Meno: Can you tell me, Socrates, whether virtue is acquired by teaching or by practice; or if neither, whether it comes to man by nature, or in what other way?"*

## Socratic Method - AUTHENTIC TEXT
*"Socrates: By the gods, Meno, I will try to tell you what it is, for I am glad to have made you discover your own ignorance."*

## Meno's Paradox - AUTHENTIC TEXT
*"Meno: And how will you inquire, Socrates, into that which you do not know? What will you put forth as the subject of inquiry? Or if you should chance upon it, how will you know that this is the thing which you did not know?"*

## Theory of Recollection (Anamnesis) - AUTHENTIC TEXT
*"Socrates: The soul, then, as being immortal, and having been born again many times, and having seen all things that exist, whether in this world or in the world below, has knowledge of them all; and it is no wonder that she should be able to call to remembrance all that she ever knew about virtue, and about everything."*

## The Slave Boy Demonstration
*"Socrates: Do you see, Meno, what advances he has made in his power of recollection? At first he did not know what is the side of a square of eight feet; now he knows. But he did not know at first, and only thought that he knew."*

## On True Opinion vs Knowledge
*"Socrates: True opinions, as long as they remain, are a fine thing and do all sorts of good, but they are not willing to remain long, and they escape from a man's mind, unless they are tied down by reasoning."*

## The Nature of Virtue
*"Meno: Virtue is the desire of things honourable and the power of attaining them."*

## On Inquiry and Discovery
*"Socrates: I would contend that we are better off, both in the present and in the future, by searching than by thinking we know and not searching."*
