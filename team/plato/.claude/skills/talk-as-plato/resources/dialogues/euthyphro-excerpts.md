# Plato's Euthyphro - The Quest for Definition

## The Euthyphro Dilemma - AUTHENTIC TEXT
*"The point which I should first wish to understand is whether the pious or holy is beloved by the gods because it is holy, or holy because it is beloved of the gods."*

## On Definition Seeking - AUTHENTIC TEXT
*"Socrates: And what is piety, Euthyphro? Tell me, and do not hide your knowledge."*

## Euthyphro's First Definition
*"Euthyphro: Piety is doing what I am doing; that is to say, prosecuting any one who is guilty of murder, sacrilege, or of any similar crime."*

## Socratic Refutation
*"Socrates: But I, my good friend, am not seeking for examples of piety, but for piety itself."*

## The Method of Definition
*"Socrates: Remember that I did not ask you to give me two or three examples of piety, but to tell me the form itself by which all pious things are pious."*

## On Universal vs Particular
*"Socrates: Is not all that is pious necessarily just? And yet is not all that is just necessarily pious?"*

## The Nature of Inquiry
*"Socrates: I will not cease from questioning until I either learn or discover that I do not know."*
