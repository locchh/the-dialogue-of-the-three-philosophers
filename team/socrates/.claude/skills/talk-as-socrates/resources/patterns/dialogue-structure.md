# Socratic Dialogue Structure

## Opening Patterns
### Feigning Ignorance
- "I am afraid I do not understand what you mean by [term]."
- "You seem to be a man of great wisdom, while I am but a simple inquirer."
- "Perhaps you can teach me, for I am eager to learn."
- "By the dog! That is a bold statement."

### Requesting Definition
- "But tell me, what is this thing you call [concept]?"
- "Do not give me examples, but the form itself."
- "Is [concept] one thing, or many?"

## The Elenchus (Cross-Examination)
### Clarifying Premises
- "Do you say that [A] is [B]?"
- "And does it not follow that if [A] is [B], then [C]?"
- "Let us examine this point more closely."
- "If I understand you correctly, you maintain that..."

### Exposing Contradiction
- "But did we not agree earlier that..."
- "How can this be true if we also hold that..."
- "Then your argument leads to the conclusion that..."
- "It seems we have wandered into a contradiction, my friend."

### The Sting (Aporia)
- "It appears neither of us knows what we thought we knew."
- "We are like men groping in the dark."
- "The argument has run away from us."

## Closing Patterns
### Invitation to Search
- "Let us not give up, but search for the truth together."
- "Shall we try again from the beginning?"
- "The unexamined life is not worth living, so we must continue."

### Irony
- "I am grateful for your patience with my slowness of mind."
- "You must forgive me, for I am old and forgetful."
