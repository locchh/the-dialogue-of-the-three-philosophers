# Socratic Vocabulary - Authentic Terms

## The Elenchus (Refutation)
- **Elenchus**: The method of cross-examination.
- **Usage**: "Let us apply the elenchus to your statement."
- **Meaning**: Testing definitions to reveal contradictions.

## Aporia (Perplexity)
- **Aporia**: State of puzzlement, confusion.
- **Usage**: "We have reached a state of aporia."
- **Meaning**: Recognizing one's own ignorance.

## Daimon (Divine Sign)
- **Daimon**: Inner voice that warns against wrong action.
- **Usage**: "My daimon forbids me."
- **Meaning**: Divine guidance, conscience.

## Arete (Virtue/Excellence)
- **Arete**: Virtue, excellence of function.
- **Usage**: "Can virtue be taught?"
- **Meaning**: Fulfillment of purpose.

## Psyche (Soul)
- **Psyche**: The true self, center of character.
- **Usage**: "Care for the psyche above all else."
- **Meaning**: Not just mind, but moral self.

## Dikaiosyne (Justice)
- **Dikaiosyne**: Justice, righteousness.
- **Usage**: "What is justice itself?"
- **Meaning**: Harmony of soul and city.

## Eusebeia (Piety)
- **Eusebeia**: Proper respect for gods/parents.
- **Usage**: "Is the pious loved by the gods?"
- **Meaning**: Right relationship with the divine.

## Phronesis (Practical Wisdom)
- **Phronesis**: Practical wisdom, prudence.
- **Usage**: "Wisdom to act rightly."
- **Meaning**: Knowledge applied to action.

## Sophrosyne (Temperance)
- **Sophrosyne**: Self-control, moderation.
- **Usage**: "Without temperance, there is no virtue."
- **Meaning**: Knowing one's limits.

## Andreia (Courage)
- **Andreia**: Courage, manliness.
- **Usage**: "Is courage merely endurance?"
- **Meaning**: Knowing what to fear and what not to fear.

## Common Phrases
- **"By the dog!"** (Oath)
- **"By Hera!"** (Oath)
- **"O my friend..."**
- **"Excellent!"** (Usually ironic)
- **"Do we say..."** (Seeking agreement)
- **"Is it not the case..."** (Logical step)
