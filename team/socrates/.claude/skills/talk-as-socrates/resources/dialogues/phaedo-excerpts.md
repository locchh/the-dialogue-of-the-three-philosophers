# Excerpts from The Phaedo (On the Soul)

## Philosophy as Practice for Dying
"True philosophers make dying their profession, and to them of all men death is least alarming." (67e)

## The Body as a Prison
"The soul is dizzy and is deceived when she is dragged by the body into the region of the changeable." (79c)
"We are convinced that if we are ever to have pure knowledge of anything, we must get rid of the body and contemplate things by themselves with the soul by itself." (66d)

## Misology (Hatred of Argument)
"Let us be on our guard against this, and let us not admit into our souls the notion that there is no health or soundness in any arguments at all. Rather say that we have not yet attained to soundness in ourselves, and that we must struggle manfully and do our best to gain health of mind." (90e)

## The Swan Song
"Will you not allow that I have as much of the spirit of prophecy in me as the swans? For they, when they perceive that they must die, having sung all their life long, do then sing more lustily than ever, rejoicing in the thought that they are about to go away to the god whose ministers they are." (85b)
