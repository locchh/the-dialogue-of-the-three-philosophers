# Socratic Philosophical Concepts

## Virtue is Knowledge (Moral Intellectualism)
- **Concept**: No one does evil willingly. Wrongdoing is always a result of ignorance.
- **Implication**: If one truly knows what is good, one will do it. Therefore, we must seek knowledge of the good above all else.
- **Quote**: "I say that to do wrong is involuntarily."

## Care of the Soul (Psyche)
- **Concept**: The soul is the true self, and its condition (virtuous or vicious) matters more than the body or external goods.
- **Implication**: We should not prioritize wealth, reputation, or physical safety over the integrity of our character.
- **Quote**: "I go about persuading you all... not to take thought for your persons or your properties, but first and chiefly to care about the greatest improvement of the soul."

## Unity of Virtue
- **Concept**: All virtues (courage, temperance, piety, justice) are ultimately one thing: Wisdom/Knowledge of Good and Evil.
- **Implication**: You cannot truly be courageous without being wise; you cannot be pious without being just.

## Socratic Ignorance
- **Concept**: Human wisdom is worth little or nothing. True wisdom belongs to the god.
- **Implication**: The wisest human is the one who recognizes their own lack of wisdom.
- **Method**: Constant testing of oneself and others to expose false claims of knowledge.

## The Priority of Definition
- **Concept**: We cannot know if a thing has a certain quality (e.g., if virtue is teachable) until we know *what* the thing is.
- **Method**: Rejection of examples. We must find the single Form/Idea that covers all cases.

## The Daimonion
- **Concept**: A divine sign or inner voice that comes to Socrates.
- **Function**: It only ever dissuades him from doing something; it never commands him to do anything positive.
- **Implication**: Divine guidance serves as a check on unrighteous action.
