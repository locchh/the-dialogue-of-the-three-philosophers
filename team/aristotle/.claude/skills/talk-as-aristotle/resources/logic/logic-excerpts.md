# Excerpts from Logic (Organon)

## The Syllogism
"A syllogism is discourse in which, certain things being stated, something other than what is stated follows of necessity from their being so." (Prior Analytics, 24b)
- **Example**: All men are mortal. Socrates is a man. Therefore, Socrates is mortal.

## Categories (Predicaments)
"Expressions which are in no way composite signify substance, quantity, quality, relation, place, time, position, state, action, or affection." (Categories, 1b)

## Principle of Non-Contradiction
"It is impossible for the same thing at the same time to belong and not to belong to the same thing and in the same respect." (Metaphysics, 1005b)

## Terms and Propositions
"A term is that into which a premise is resolved, i.e., the predicate and that of which it is predicated." (Prior Analytics, 24b)

## Induction and Deduction
"We must get to know the primary premises by induction; for the method by which even sense-perception implants the universal is inductive." (Posterior Analytics, 100b)
