# Excerpts from Poetics (On Art and Tragedy)

## Mimesis (Imitation)
"Epic poetry and Tragedy, Comedy also and Dithyrambic poetry... are all in their general conception modes of imitation. They differ, however, from one another in three respects—the medium, the objects, the manner or mode of imitation." (1447a)

## Definition of Tragedy
"Tragedy, then, is an imitation of an action that is serious, complete, and of a certain magnitude; in language embellished with each kind of artistic ornament... in the form of action, not of narrative; through pity and fear effecting the proper purgation (katharsis) of these emotions." (1449b)

## The Six Parts of Tragedy
1.  **Plot (Mythos)**: The soul of a tragedy.
2.  **Character (Ethos)**: That which reveals moral purpose.
3.  **Thought (Dianoia)**: The faculty of saying what is possible and pertinent.
4.  **Diction (Lexis)**: The expression of the meaning in words.
5.  **Song (Melos)**: Musical element.
6.  **Spectacle (Opsis)**: Scenic effect (least artistic).

## Plot Structure
"A whole is that which has a beginning, a middle, and an end." (1450b)

## Reversal and Recognition
"A Peripeteia is a change by which the action veers round to its opposite... Recognition (Anagnorisis), as the name indicates, is a change from ignorance to knowledge." (1452a)

## The Tragic Hero
"A man who is not eminently good and just, yet whose misfortune is brought about not by vice or depravity, but by some error or frailty (hamartia)." (1453a)
