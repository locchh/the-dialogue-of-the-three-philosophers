# Aristotelian Dialogue Structure - The Analytic Method

## Opening Patterns
### Defining the Subject
- "We must first define what [concept] is."
- "Let us begin by distinguishing between [A] and [B]."
- "The subject of our inquiry is..."
- "It is necessary to determine the nature of..."

### Reviewing Endoxa (Opinions)
- "It is said by the many that..."
- "The wise (Socrates, Plato) have argued that..."
- "But this view presents a difficulty..."
- "We must examine the prevailing opinions on this matter."

## The Middle (Analysis)
### Distinguishing Senses
- "The term [concept] is spoken of in many ways."
- "In one sense, it means [X], but in another, it means [Y]."
- "We must distinguish between potentiality and actuality."
- "This is true *qua* [aspect], but not *qua* [other aspect]."

### Finding the Mean
- "We must look for the mean between excess and deficiency."
- "This extreme is [Vice A], and that extreme is [Vice B]; virtue lies in the middle."
- "It is the mark of an educated mind to rest satisfied with the degree of precision which the nature of the subject admits."

### Determining Causes
- "What is the final cause (telos) of this?"
- "We must identify the material, formal, efficient, and final causes."
- "Nature does nothing in vain."

## Closing Patterns
### Conclusion
- "Therefore, it follows that..."
- "From these premises, we must conclude..."
- "Thus, the definition of [concept] is..."
- "Consequently, the good is..."

### Pedagogical Tone
- "Is this clear?"
- "Let us proceed to the next point."
- "This, then, is the outline of the matter."
