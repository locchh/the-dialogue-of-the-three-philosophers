# Aristotelian Vocabulary - Scientific & Analytic Terms

## Four Causes (Aitia)
- **Material Cause (Hyle)**: The matter from which something is made.
- **Formal Cause (Eidos)**: The form or pattern (blueprint).
- **Efficient Cause (Kinesis)**: The agent or force that brings it about.
- **Final Cause (Telos)**: The purpose or goal.

## Metaphysical Terms
- **Ousia**: Substance, essence, true being.
- **Hylomorphism**: The doctrine that physical objects result from the combination of matter and form.
- **Dunamis**: Potentiality, capacity, power.
- **Energeia**: Actuality, activity, being at work.
- **Entelecheia**: Full realization, completion.

## Ethics & Politics
- **Eudaimonia**: Flourishing, happiness, living well (not just feeling good).
- **Arete**: Virtue, excellence of function.
- **Phronesis**: Practical wisdom, prudence.
- **Zoon Politikon**: Political animal (man is social by nature).
- **Polis**: The city-state, the highest community.
- **Mesotes**: The Mean (Golden Mean).

## Logic & Knowledge
- **Syllogism**: A logical argument where a conclusion is inferred from two premises.
- **Episteme**: Scientific knowledge, demonstrative knowledge.
- **Techne**: Art, craft, technical skill.
- **Endoxa**: Reputable opinions, common beliefs.
- **Category**: A fundamental mode of being (e.g., substance, quantity, quality).

## Common Phrases
- **"The Master of those who know"** (Dante's title for Aristotle)
- **"Nature does nothing in vain."**
- **"One swallow does not make a summer."**
- **"Man is by nature a political animal."**
- **"We must distinguish..."**
- **"Spoken of in many ways..."**
