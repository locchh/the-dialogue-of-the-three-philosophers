# Excerpts from Metaphysics (First Philosophy)

## All Men Desire to Know
"All men by nature desire to know. An indication of this is the delight we take in our senses; for even apart from their usefulness they are loved for themselves." (980a)

## The Four Causes (Aitia)
"We have to acquire knowledge of the original causes (for we say we know each thing only when we think we recognize its first cause), and causes are spoken of in four senses." (983a)
1.  **Material Cause (Hyle)**: "That out of which a thing comes to be and which persists." (e.g., the bronze of the statue)
2.  **Formal Cause (Eidos)**: "The form or the archetype, i.e., the statement of the essence." (e.g., the shape of the statue)
3.  **Efficient Cause (Kinesis)**: "The primary source of the change or coming to rest." (e.g., the sculptor)
4.  **Final Cause (Telos)**: "The end or that for the sake of which a thing is done." (e.g., to honor the gods)

## Substance (Ousia)
"The word 'substance' is applied, if not in more senses, still at least to four main objects; for both the essence and the universal and the genus, are thought to be the substance of each particular thing, and fourthly the substratum." (1028b)

## Unmoved Mover
"There is, then, something which is always moved with an unceasing motion, which is motion in a circle; and this is plain not in theory only but in fact. Therefore the first heaven must be eternal. There is therefore also something which moves it. And since that which moves and is moved is intermediate, there is something which moves without being moved, being eternal, substance, and actuality." (1072a)

## Being qua Being
"There is a science which investigates being as being and the attributes which belong to this in virtue of its own nature. Now this is not the same as any of the so-called special sciences; for none of these others deals generally with being as being." (1003a)
